Innate approach

How does the theory works?
  • The theory that suggests that human beings are pre-programmed to learn language
  • caregiver does not actively teach infant language
  • Language acquisition device (LAD) is a theoretical component
  • Hypothetical tool in the brain that helps children to “quickly learn and understand language.”
  • The brain can categorize lexemes into nouns/verbs/adjectives/other word classes from a very early age
Poverty of the stimulus
Poverty of the stimulus argues for the innate approach, it suggests that children are not exposed to rich enough data within their linguistic environment to acquire every feature of the language.
The argument from poverty of the stimulus takes the following structure:
  1. The speech children are exposed to is consistent with numerous possible grammars
  2. It is possible to define data D,that would distinguish the target grammar from all other grammars that are consistent with the input
  3. D is missing from speech of children
  4. Children acquire it nonetheless
  5. Therefore, the right grammatical structure arises due to (possibly linguistic) property of the child
Ideas/Quotes
“The constraint that’s keeping a chimpanzee from speaking or indeed keeping a dog from speaking is not the peripheral vocal anatomy. Because any of these animals that we’ve looked at are able to lower the larynx and reconfigure the peripheral vocal tract into a human-like configuration, basically anytime they vocalize” - Tecumseh Fitch (2009)

The anatomy of humans and other mammals (such as primates and even dogs) is so similar that by process of elimination, it must be a matter of cognitive ability.
