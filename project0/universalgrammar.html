Empiricist VS Rationalist

Empricist approach

The empiricist approach suggests that language depends on the environment and that language cannot be learnt without the environment. Chomsky argues against the point above by pointing out a distinction in language. There is linguistic performance and linguistic competence. Linguistic competence is the knowledge of grammar within a language while linguistic performance is the application of this knowledge to acquire language.

Rationalist approach

Chomsky claims that the child's ability to acquire language is based on a knowledge of the universal grammatical principles that the child is born with.Chomsky states that human language relies on a knowledge of the structural dependence of language. This approach to language that chomsky took is categorized as the rationalist approach to language acquisition.

Rationalists argument

The rationalist questions the empirical approach by questioning what the child hears. For example, in everyday language the child would hear false starts and only certain aspects of the language. In other words, the child would have an incomplete view on how the language works. The child should only be able to develop language based on an already understood principal of language structure.
